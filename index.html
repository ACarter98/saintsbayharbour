Guernsey's Most Picturesque Fishing Harbour

A charming working harbour on the south coast, where local fishermen land fresh catch daily and visitors enjoy coastal walks.

About Saints Bay Harbour

Saints Bay Harbour represents authentic Guernsey coastal life. Unlike the developed west coast beaches, this small working harbour remains largely unchanged for generations. Colourful fishing boats bob in the sheltered water, their owners continuing traditions that stretch back centuries.

The harbour was built in the 19th century to serve the local fishing fleet and provide shelter from sudden Channel storms. Its position, tucked into a natural cove, offers protection from most wind directions - essential for small boats in these waters.

Fresh Fish Daily

Local fishermen still land their catch here. Early mornings see boats returning with bass, mackerel, and lobster. Some fishermen sell direct from the harbour - ask around, but respect that these are working people, not tourist attractions.

Coastal Gateway

The harbour marks the start (or end) of some of Guernsey's finest coastal walks. The path east leads to Icart Point and stunning cliff scenery. West takes you to Moulin Huet and the Pea Stacks. The Saints Bay Inn provides welcome refreshment.

Visitor Tips

  • Arrive before 8am to see catch being landed
  • Speak to fishermen respectfully if buying fish
  • Harbour wall is slippery when wet - take care
  • Saints Bay Inn serves excellent seafood
  • Check tide times - harbour empties completely
  • No ATM nearby - bring cash for fish purchases
